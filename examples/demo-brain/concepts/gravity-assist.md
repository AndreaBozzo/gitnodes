A gravity assist (or "slingshot") steals a tiny amount of a planet's orbital
momentum to change a spacecraft's speed and direction without spending fuel. Fly
behind a moving planet and you leave faster than you arrived; fly in front and
you slow down.

It is what makes the outer solar system reachable on a realistic fuel budget.
[Lodestar](../missions/lodestar.md) uses a [Jupiter](../bodies/jupiter.md) pass
to bend onto its survey trajectory, and Saturn-bound flights chain assists to
arrive without an impractically large rocket.
