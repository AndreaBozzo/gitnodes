type: concept
title: Radioisotope power
tags: [engineering]
---

Beyond the asteroid belt, sunlight is too weak for solar panels to power a
spacecraft reliably. A radioisotope thermoelectric generator instead turns the
steady heat of decaying plutonium-238 directly into electricity, with no moving
parts, running for decades.

Every Meridian spacecraft that ventures past Mars carries one — including the
[Helios Relay](../spacecraft/helios-relay.md), which must keep its transmitter
warm and powered through years of cruise.
