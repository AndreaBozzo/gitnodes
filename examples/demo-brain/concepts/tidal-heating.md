title: Tidal heating
tags: [science, ocean-world]
---

Tidal heating is the friction warmth a moon generates as a giant planet's gravity
stretches and squeezes it on every orbit. The flexing turns gravitational energy
into heat deep inside the moon — enough, in places, to melt ice into liquid water.

It is the reason ocean worlds exist far from the Sun: tidal heating from
[Jupiter](../bodies/jupiter.md) keeps [Europa](../bodies/europa.md)'s subsurface
ocean liquid, the central question the [Tidewater](../missions/tidewater.md)
mission was built to answer.
