For decades, [Europa](../bodies/europa.md)'s ocean was an inference from magnetic
data. [Tidewater](../missions/tidewater.md)'s ice-penetrating radar made it a
measurement: clear echoes from a sharp boundary roughly fifteen kilometres down,
exactly where ice would meet liquid water. The plume sampler then caught salts
and organics venting from below — the first direct chemistry from another world's
ocean, and the program's headline result.
