Mars is the program's first and closest target: a cold, dry world with a thin
carbon-dioxide atmosphere, ancient river valleys, and seasonal deposits of frost
at both poles. Its tenuous air is just thick enough to brake an incoming orbiter
yet too thin to land large payloads easily.

The [Ares Survey](../missions/ares-survey.md) mission maps its surface
mineralogy from orbit, hunting for the clay and sulphate signatures that mark
where liquid water once stood.
