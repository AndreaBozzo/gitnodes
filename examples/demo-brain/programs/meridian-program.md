The Meridian Program is a fifteen-year international effort to survey the most
promising worlds of the inner and outer solar system with a shared robotic
fleet. Rather than fly one-off missions, Meridian pools a common spacecraft bus,
a single deep-space [communications relay](../spacecraft/helios-relay.md), and an
open data archive across every flight.

Four flagship missions are in flight or in build: [Ares Survey](../missions/ares-survey.md)
at Mars, [Tidewater](../missions/tidewater.md) at Europa, [Skyhook](../missions/skyhook.md)
at Titan, and [Lodestar](../missions/lodestar.md) on its way through the Jupiter
system. Every mission is required to publish its calibrated data within six months
of downlink — the archive is the point, not any single spacecraft.
