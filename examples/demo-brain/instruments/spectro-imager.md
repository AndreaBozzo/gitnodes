VIRSI is an imaging spectrometer: a camera that records hundreds of wavelengths
per pixel, so each image is also a mineral map. Different minerals absorb light
at telltale wavelengths, letting the instrument pick out clays and sulphates —
the fingerprints of past liquid water — from orbit around [Mars](../bodies/mars.md).
